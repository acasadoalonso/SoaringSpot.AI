# IGC Champion Pilot of the Year (World Soaring Cup)

This award was created by the FAI Gliding Commission (IGC) to recognise the top performance in FAI World Gliding Championships. Each Championship calendar year, the FAI World Champion with the highest score is named the **IGC Champion Pilot of the Year** and is also awarded the **World Soaring Cup**.

-----

## The Calculation

The IGC Champion Pilot of the Year is awarded to the gliding world champion, from among all classes, who earned the highest final score **divided by the maximum possible score** for the championship they flew in that year.

```
ratio = pilot's final score / maximum possible score for that championship
```

The pilot with the **highest ratio across all World Championship classes** that year wins.

-----

## Eligibility

You must be a **World Champion** (1st place in your class at a WGC held that calendar year). The calculation rules are based on the winning scores of all World Gliding Championships of the calendar year.

In years with multiple WGCs (Open, 18m, 20m, Standard, Club, 15m, Women’s, Junior…), all class winners are compared using that same percentage formula, and the one with the highest ratio takes the title.

-----

## The Trophy

The World Soaring Cup is awarded to the new IGC Champion Pilot of the Year as soon as practical at the conclusion of the final WGC of the Championship Calendar Year. The winner has two options:

- Have the WSC **shipped by FAI** (Home Office in Lausanne)
- Receive it at the **IGC Plenary meeting** (normally held in February/March)

-----

## Summary

> **Win your class at a WGC, score the highest percentage of maximum points among all class winners that year — and the trophy is yours.**
